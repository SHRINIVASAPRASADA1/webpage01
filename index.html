Got A Question

Answers, right here.

Is there a processing fee on the education loan?

Yes — depending on the loan amount, the lender may levy a processing fee, typically up to 2%, included within the disclosed Annual Percentage Rate.

Who can apply for a GDFCone-facilitated education loan?

Any Indian citizen who has secured — or is in the process of securing — admission to a recognised institution in India or abroad.

What is the minimum CIBIL score needed?

Most lenders expect a co-applicant CIBIL score of at least 700 for smooth processing. A score above 750 improves both approval speed and the rate offered. Below 680, select NBFCs still consider applications based on income stability.

Can I get a loan without collateral?

Yes. Several NBFCs and specialised lenders offer unsecured loans up to ₹40–75 Lakh depending on university ranking and course employability, generally at a 1.5–3% higher rate than secured loans.

How long does approval actually take?

Digital-first NBFCs typically sanction in 7–15 working days for clean files. Public sector banks usually take 4–6 weeks. Add 2–3 weeks for disbursement coordination with your university.

What expenses does the loan cover?

Tuition, examination fees, living expenses, travel, insurance, study equipment, and pre-admission costs — disbursed in tranches aligned to your academic calendar, often direct to the university in foreign currency.

Who can be my co-applicant?

Typically a parent, sibling, parent-in-law or spouse — and with certain NBFC partners, a wider circle of relatives than banks usually permit.

Do I repay while I'm still studying?

Most lenders offer a moratorium covering your full course plus 6–12 months after. Simple interest accrues during this period; paying it as you go reduces your total repayment burden.
